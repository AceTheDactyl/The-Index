Self Referential Category Theoretic Structures

Author: Kael
Experimental

📜 Overview

An exploration of category theoretic structures that exhibit self-reference, recursion, and fixed-point semantics. This system investigates the mathematical foundations where categories can model their own structure, leading to insights in type theory, logic, and foundational mathematics.

Key areas of investigation include endofunctors, fixed-point combinators, recursive types, and the categorical semantics of self-referential systems.

🧰 Core Concepts

Endofunctors

Functors from a category to itself, enabling self-mapping structures.

Fixed Points

Solutions to F(X) = X; the heart of recursive definitions.

Initial Algebras

Categorical semantics for inductive data types.

Terminal Coalgebras

Dual structures modeling coinductive and infinite data.

Recursive Types

Type systems with self-referential type definitions.

Y Combinator

Fixed-point combinator enabling anonymous recursion.
