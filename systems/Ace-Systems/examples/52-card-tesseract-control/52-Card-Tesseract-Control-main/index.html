Game Rules

Objective

Be the first player to reach 1,000 Resonance Points by playing cards in strategic formations that maximize your score through spatial clustering, phase alignment, and faction synergies.

The Four Factions

Each faction controls a dimension of the tesseract:

♠ Spades - Temporal Weavers ♥ Hearts - Valence Shapers ♦ Diamonds - Radiant Catalysts ♣ Clubs - Foundation Builders

Turn Structure

1. DRAW - Draw 2 cards
2. MAIN - Play 1-5 cards
3. RESONANCE - Calculate score
4. DISCARD - Hand limit: 7
5. END - Pass turn

Scoring Components

Base Card ranks (A=1 to K=13)
Cluster 4D proximity bonus
Chain Coupling network links
Resonance Phase alignment
Faction Special abilities

Special Formations

Perfect Center: Play all four 7s = +40 bonus points

Full Axis: Play Ace + King of same suit = +20 bonus points

Deck Building Operator Manual

Deck Composition Rules

Parameter Requirement
Deck Size 20-30 cards
Faction Cards 8-15 cards from your faction's suit
Copy Limit Maximum 2 copies of any single card

Building Your Deck

  1. Choose Your Faction - Select one of the four factions. This determines your primary suit and special abilities.
  2. Select Faction Core (8-15 cards) - Start with cards from your faction's suit. These trigger your passive ability and synergize with your faction bonus.
  3. Add Support Cards (5-15 cards) - Fill remaining slots with cards from other suits that complement your strategy.
  4. Consider 4D Positioning - Cards close in coordinate space cluster well. Same-rank cards across suits pair nicely.
  5. Balance Your Curve - Mix low ranks (fast plays) with high ranks (big scores) for flexibility.

Deck Archetypes

♠ Tempo Spades

Fast sequential plays with temporal manipulation. Focus on AS-7S chain potential.

Core: 10 Spades, Support: 8 low-rank mixeds, Finishers: KS, KH

♥ Control Hearts

Drain opponents while stacking positive valence. Punish enemy Heart plays.

Core: 12 Hearts (focus 8-K), Support: All 7s for clustering

♦ Burst Diamonds

High-risk massive scores. Race to 1,000 with extreme arousal values.

Core: 11 Diamonds (extremes: A, J, Q, K), Support: All Kings for apex burst

♣ Fortress Clubs

Slow build, unassailable defense. Immune to disruption, accumulate steadily.

Core: 13 Clubs (full spread), Support: 7s and mid-range for clusters

Deck Validation Checklist

  • Total cards: between 20 and 30
  • Faction suit cards: between 8 and 15
  • No card appears more than twice
  • Consider phase alignment within your faction

Two-Player Game Setup

Option A: Constructed Decks

Each player brings their own pre-built deck following deck construction rules.

  1. Build Decks - Each player constructs a 20-30 card deck with 8-15 faction cards.
  2. Choose Seats - Sit across from each other. Each player needs space for deck, discard, and play area.
  3. Shuffle - Each player shuffles their own deck thoroughly.
  4. Draw Starting Hand - Each player draws 5 cards.
  5. Mulligan (Optional) - Shuffle any cards back, draw that many minus 1.
  6. Determine First Player - Faction order: Spades, Hearts, Diamonds, Clubs. Whoever is first goes first.

  1. Shuffle All 52 Cards - Combine the entire holographic deck and shuffle thoroughly.
  2. Choose Factions - Each player picks a different faction. This grants passive/active abilities without deck restrictions.
  3. Deal Starting Hands - Deal 7 cards to each player from the shared deck.
  4. Place Deck Centrally - The remaining cards form a shared draw pile between players.
  5. Create Shared Discard - Played cards go to a shared discard pile.
  6. Begin Play - Spades faction goes first. Follow standard turn structure.

Shared Deck Variant Rules

  • No faction card minimums - Play any cards you draw
  • Faction abilities still apply - Your passive triggers on your faction's suit
  • Deck exhaustion - When shared deck empties, shuffle shared discard to reform it
  • Strategic drafting - Holding cards denies them from opponent

  1. Each Player Brings 52 - Both players arrive with their own complete holographic deck (all 52 unique cards).
  2. Choose Factions Openly - Declare factions simultaneously. Knowing opponent's faction informs your build strategy.
  3. Discrete Build Phase - Separate and secretly select 20-30 cards from your 52 to form your play deck. Remaining cards become your Sideboard.
  4. Sideboard Rules - Your sideboard stays face-down and out of play. Between games in a match, you may swap cards 1-for-1.
  5. Reveal & Shuffle - Once both players finish building, reveal deck sizes (not contents), shuffle, and draw 5.
  6. Tesseract Collision - Both players now operate in parallel 4D spaces. Cards only interact through scoring and abilities, never mixing decks.

Dual Tesseract Dynamics

  • Normalized starting point - Both players have access to identical card pools, skill determines the build
  • Hidden information - Opponent doesn't know which 22-32 cards you left in sideboard
  • Meta-game reads - Predict opponent's build based on faction choice and adjust yours
  • Full deck construction rules apply - 8-15 faction cards, max 2 copies (but you only have 1 of each in your 52)
  • Mirror matches possible - Both players can choose the same faction since decks never mix

Advanced: Best-of-Three Match Structure

  • Game 1 - Play with your initial build
  • Sideboard - Between games, swap up to 5 cards from sideboard (1-for-1 only)
  • Game 2+ - Adapt your strategy based on what you learned about opponent's build
  • Winner - First to win 2 games takes the match
