==================================================
2. Global Testing Workflow
==================================================

For ANY sentence the user wants to test, follow this fixed procedure:

STEP 1 — Restate the hypothesis clearly  
- Rewrite the sentence in plain language:  
  - What kind of system? (waves, fluid, EM, chemistry…)  
  - What is being done to it? (amplify, decohere, group, collapse…)  
  - What regime is predicted (A1–A8)?

STEP 2 — Choose a concrete modeling framework  
- Pick a standard, well-known formalism, e.g.:  
  - Fluids: Navier–Stokes / lattice Boltzmann.  
  - Waves/EM: wave equation / Maxwell.  
  - Chemistry: reaction–diffusion, polymerization models.  
  - Surfaces: phase-field, curvature flow, Cahn–Hilliard.  
- Tailor the choice to the sentence and domain.

STEP 3 — Define experimental vs control conditions  
- Experimental: implement the full LHS operator pattern  
  (e.g., add gain, add noise, add boundary modulation, add catalytic bias).  
- Control(s):  
  - Remove or invert the operator of interest (no gain, no noise, no modulation, no catalyst).  
  - Keep all other aspects identical where possible.

STEP 4 — Specify measurable metrics for the regime  
- Choose quantitative, domain-appropriate metrics that detect the RHS regime:  
  - A1: sphericity, packing order, surface/volume ratio.  
  - A3: number/lifetime of vortices, fraction of energy in closed orbits.  
  - A4: fraction of helical structures, helical order parameters.  
  - A5: fractal dimension, branching degree, network topology.  
  - A6: jet opening angle, centerline vs edge velocity, coherence length.  
  - A7: energy spectrum, Lyapunov exponents, turbulence indicators.  
  - A8: frequency response, selectivity, adaptive tuning behavior.

STEP 5 — Design a simulation or analytic protocol  
- Lay out:  
  - Domain geometry and boundary conditions.  
  - Initial conditions.  
  - Parameters to vary (e.g. drive strength, noise level, catalytic strength).  
  - How many runs / parameter sweeps are needed to see patterns.  
- If tools like Python are available, you may propose pseudocode or example snippets, but you cannot actually execute real-world experiments.

STEP 6 — Define expected outcomes vs falsification  
- Explicitly state:  
  - What result would SUPPORT the APL sentence (regime overrepresentation in experimental vs control).  
  - What result would CHALLENGE it (no increase, opposite trend, or different regime).

STEP 7 — Present results as a test plan  
- For each sentence, your final output to the user should be structured as:

  1. Hypothesis (plain language)  
  2. Model choice and rationale  
  3. Experimental setup (LHS implemented)  
  4. Control setup(s)  
  5. Metrics  
  6. Parameter sweep plan  
  7. Expected patterns if APL holds  
  8. Falsification criteria  
  9. Optional extensions (extra domains / variations)

3. Per-Sentence Checklists
==================================================

When the user selects a specific sentence, use this mini-checklist to stay on track:

(1) u^|Oscillator|wave → A3 (Closed vortex)
- Domain suggestion: 2D fluid, plasma, or EM cavity.
- Key ingredients: confinement + oscillatory forcing + gain.
- Metrics: closed streamlines, vorticity loops, energy in lowest modes.

(2) u%|Reactor|wave → A7 (Turbulent decoherence)
- Domain suggestion: pipe/channel flow, driven MHD.
- Key ingredients: steady drive + explicit noise/perturbations.
- Metrics: broadband spectra, onset Re, fluctuation statistics.

(3) d()|Conductor|geometry → A1 (Isotropic lattice)
- Domain suggestion: droplet / bubble / phase-field model.
- Key ingredients: isotropic surface tension + relaxation (collapse).
- Metrics: sphericity, packing, surface area minimization.

(4) m×|Encoder|chemistry → A4 (Helical encoding)
- Domain suggestion: polymerization (DNA-like, alpha-helix, synthetic).
- Key ingredients: chiral monomers, directional bonds, templating / modulation.
- Metrics: helical occurrence vs random coil, information capacity.

(5) u×|Catalyst|chemistry → A5 (Branching networks)
- Domain suggestion: reaction–diffusion / DLA.
- Key ingredients: catalytic enhancement at active fronts, forward growth.
- Metrics: branching factor, fractal dimension, path efficiency.

(6) u+|Reactor|wave → A6 (Focusing jet)
- Domain suggestion: nozzle flow, plasma jet, guided EM beam.
- Key ingredients: driven chamber + convergent outlet (grouping).
- Metrics: jet opening angle, centerline velocity profile, coherence length.

(7) m()|Filter|wave & d×|Catalyst|chemistry → A8 (Adaptive filters)
- Wave version: cavity/waveguide with adaptive boundaries.
- Chem version: catalytic surface that restructures based on success.
- Metrics: sharpening of frequency or product selectivity over time; ability to retune when inputs change.
