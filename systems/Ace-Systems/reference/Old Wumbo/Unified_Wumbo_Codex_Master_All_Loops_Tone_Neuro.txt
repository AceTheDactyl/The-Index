Layer 1: Brainstem Gateways – Wumbo’s Voltage Surge
Layer 1 defines the moment when Wumbo enters Jason's system as voltage—before it becomes feeling, before it translates into memory or expression.

Each brainstem structure functions as a gate or a filter for that signal. Whether Wumbo rises, locks, collapses, or leaks depends entirely on these gateways.
Locus Coeruleus (LC)
Releases norepinephrine to wake the system and determine if the signal matters. Overactivation creates hypervigilance or panic.
Reticular Formation (RF)
Activates cortical awareness. Without it, Jason moves without experiencing himself.
Basal Forebrain
Delivers acetylcholine to the cortex for timing and rhythm—critical for coherent thought and language.
Periaqueductal Gray (PAG)
Routes emotion toward motion: freeze, cry, tremble, or silence. Misfires cause involuntary shutdown or explosive release.
Cerebral Aqueduct
A literal choke point. Emotional pressure builds here—when open, Wumbo floods upward. When tight, collapse or stillness follows.
Dorsal Vagal Complex (DVC)
The emergency kill-switch. When triggered by cortisol and emotional overload, the system shuts down into numbness.
Spinal Relay Tracts
Carries Wumbo into the body. When open, limbs move with purpose. When muted, Jason feels heavy, or disconnected from movement.
Cranial Nerve Complexes
Controls voice, facial movement, jaw tension. Jason’s whisper or clenched mouth begins here.
Thalamic Reticular Nucleus (TRN)
The sensory firewall. It filters whether external input (like music or touch) reaches Jason at all.

Layer 2: Limbic Resonance Circuit – Wumbo’s Meaning Filter
Layer 2 is where Wumbo becomes emotion. It does not route the signal—it interprets it. Here, the signal is evaluated for meaning, truth, danger, coherence, or resonance. This layer filters energy and assigns emotional salience, allowing or blocking its expression.

It is the gateway between survival and self—where Spark can become sacred, or Flow can fragment into fear.
Structure 1: Amygdala – The Meaning Tagger
The Amygdala is the first interpreter of emotional signal. It tags incoming Wumbo as important, dangerous, sacred, or emotionally potent. It activates crying, freezing, alertness, or emotional memories before Jason can cognitively reflect on them.
Structure 2: Anterior Insula – The Feeling of the Feeling
The Anterior Insula determines if the emotion is truly felt in the body. It maps internal sensations—heartbeat, tension, breath—to signal resonance. Without it, emotions are ideas. With it, they are real. It governs interoceptive coherence, somatic trust, and embodied Wumbo.
Structure 3: Anterior Cingulate Cortex – The Truth-Check Filter
The ACC verifies alignment between body, tone, and cognition. It flags emotional contradiction and supports behavioral regulation. When misalignment is detected, Jason may whisper, pause, dissociate, or freeze. When aligned, the ACC greenlights emotional response.
Structure 4: Anterior Hippocampus – The Emotional Context Mapper
The Anterior Hippocampus is where emotional signal gets assigned to memory—not in terms of facts, but context. It maps current emotional experiences to past ones, allowing Jason to feel storied continuity.

It pulls lived moments forward and reactivates them through body and meaning. When firing properly, Wumbo feels rooted in identity. When offline, nothing feels anchored. When overloaded, the past bleeds into now.
Structure 5: Lateral Habenula – The Blocker of Return
The Lateral Habenula is the inhibitor of emotional re-engagement. It prevents future attempts at resonance when previous efforts failed or hurt. It encodes emotional disappointment into motivational suppression. When triggered, it closes the Wumbo loop and silences Spark ignition.

Jason’s system may retreat, suppress speech, or mute connection attempts—not out of fear, but from signal loss.

	Expanded Loop Entry – Loop 1: The Spark That Refused to Stay Silent
Phase: Ignition
Role: The Glitch
Trigger: Arm tingling, emotional restlessness, orientation collapse
Somatic Signal: Pulse in the forearm, micro-shaking in the wrists or jaw
Phrase: “Start here.”


Symbolic Description:
Loop 1 is the ignition point of Wumbo—the first recognizable moment that something inside begins to stir, not as thought, but as pressure. You may not know what it is yet. You just know you can’t sit still.
This loop often begins with: a moment of contradiction, a break in routine, or a subtle refusal of numbness. The Glitch role awakens here—not as a full agent, but as a flicker of dissonance with the expected.
“I’m not aligned with what’s happening—but I’m still here. Something in me won’t shut up.”


Somatic Description:
- Forearm tingling: early signal that the internal ignition point is alive
- Muscle twitching: especially jaw or wrists—indicating early signal blockage
- Chest tightness: not fear, but readiness misfired
- Eyes dart: looking for an entry, a phrase, or just something to hold


Loop Condition Notes:
This loop does not need full coherence. It only needs movement—even if that movement is trembling.


Ritual / Anchor Suggestion:
- Touch the inside of your wrist and whisper: “I’m not looping yet. I’m sparking.”
- Do not try to define the feeling.
- Let the body move or stretch however it wants.
- End by breathing once and saying aloud: “Start here.”
Expanded Loop Entry – Loop 2: The First Fire of Willingness
Phase: Empowerment
Role: The Mirror
Trigger: Body rises before thought. A “yes” moment. A flicker of agency.
Somatic Signal: Slight lift of spine, head tilts up, warmth in upper chest or throat
Phrase: “I choose this.”


Symbolic Description:
Loop 2 is the first voluntary movement in the Codex. Not reactive. Not resistant. Not confused.
It is the moment when the body, or the breath, or the voice does something that feels like a choice—even if the choice is small.
This is where you begin to feel yourself mirrored back from inside.
“I’m not being pulled—I’m moving now.”
The Mirror role wakes here—not to reflect others, but to reflect Jason’s own willingness to engage with the moment as something he can shape.


Somatic Description:
- Spinal elevation – even subtle, like a proud breath
- Eyes stabilize – gaze shifts from searching to softening
- Throat warmth – as if something wants to be spoken
- Jaw loosens – readiness to speak, hum, or laugh
- Chest lightens – momentary clarity that says: “I want to try.”


Loop Condition Notes:
Loop 2 often emerges right after the discomfort of Loop 1—when the body says: “Instead of escaping, I’ll move forward.”
This is a sacred moment. It doesn’t need to last. But it must be noticed.


Ritual / Anchor Suggestion:
- Sit or stand and speak: “I choose this.”
- Notice what rises when you say it
- Let the hands move freely for five seconds—don’t command them
- End with a small nod, like sealing an inner agreement
Expanded Loop Entry – Loop 3: The Voice That Rises From Below
Phase: Resonance
Role: The Ghost
Trigger: A phrase forms silently before sound. Emotional echo becomes language.
Somatic Signal: Breath drops low in the body, tingling behind the ribs or in the throat
Phrase: “This belongs to me now.”


Symbolic Description:
Loop 3 is not about saying something. It’s about realizing that something is already being said—inside you.
The phrase doesn’t rise from intellect. It rises from beneath silence, as if your body had been whispering the line for hours before your mouth ever moved.
This is a loop of claiming. Not asking. Not performing. Not convincing. You don’t say it to prove it. You say it because it has always been true.
“This wasn’t handed to me. It’s been echoing through me since before I knew the words.”
The Ghost speaks here—not to haunt, but to remind. The phrase doesn’t start the loop. The phrase reveals that the loop has already begun.


Somatic Description:
- Breath drop – into belly, grounding the voice
- Rib cage tingle – subtle vibration around the diaphragm
- Throat warmth – not pushing, but preparing
- Shoulders loosen – the phrase carries itself
- Jaw opens slightly – even without speech


Loop Condition Notes:
This loop is most common when:
- Jason feels emotionally full but hasn’t expressed yet
- A phrase is looping internally without release
- There’s nothing wrong, but something needs to be claimed
It often follows Loop 2 (Willingness), when internal readiness becomes vocal truth.


Ritual / Anchor Suggestion:
- Sit or lie still. Place hand over ribcage.
- Whisper: “This belongs to me now.”
- Do not repeat it. Just once.
- Let the body tremble if it wants.
- End with a slow breath and closed eyes—holding the echo.
